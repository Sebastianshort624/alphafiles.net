Mutual Release Letter
What it is, what it says, and what it means for you
A Mutual Release is a signed legal document between you and the resort. It is the only outcome that ends this completely — permanently removing your name from the deed and terminating all financial obligations.
Example Release Language
Example — Mutual Release & Termination Agreement

This Mutual Release and Termination Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] by and between [RESORT NAME] ("Developer") and [CLIENT NAME] ("Owner").

WHEREAS, Owner holds an interest in [RESORT] pursuant to a Timeshare Purchase Agreement dated [DATE];

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, Developer and Owner agree as follows:

1. Termination. The timeshare interest is hereby terminated, cancelled, and of no further force or effect. Owner is released from all obligations arising from said interest, including but not limited to maintenance fees, assessments, and any mortgage obligation held by Developer.

2. Deed Reconveyance. Developer shall record a deed or instrument sufficient to remove Owner's name from title at [COUNTY] County within 30 days of execution.

3. Mutual Release. Each party releases the other from any and all claims, known or unknown, arising from the ownership interest described herein.

How the Deed Is Removed
✍️
Release Signed
Both parties execute the Mutual Release agreement
🏛️
Filed at County
Recorded at the county where the resort is located — typically within 30 days
✅
Title Cleared
Your name is permanently removed from the deed and all county records
Timeline
With mortgage: Average 6–18 months from case acceptance to signed release.
Paid off: Generally resolves faster — no lender coordination required.
Guarantee: If no Favorable Outcome within 36 months, full refund or free extension.
