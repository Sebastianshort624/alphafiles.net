Resort Relentless Pursuit

From the first missed payment to garnished wages — what this looks like, start to finish

Jason and Andrea,

I want to make sure you have a clear picture of what the resort will do if you decide to simply stop paying maintenance fees — even though your mortgage is paid off. A lot of owners assume being paid in full puts them in a safer position. It does not.

The moment you miss a payment the resort begins automated calls, certified letters, and past-due notices. Within 30 days all three credit bureaus are notified — Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion simultaneously — and most owners see a 100–150 point credit drop almost immediately. Shortly after, the account is sold to a collections agency, which adds a second independent negative mark to your report. Formal foreclosure notices follow in writing. None of this is a bluff. Here is where it goes from there.

Where it splits — determined by your age and financial profile
Near or at retirement age (55+)

Resorts often skip full foreclosure — not out of mercy, but strategy. Instead they destroy the credit, keep collections active, and wait. A timeshare is real property attached to your estate. When you pass, your heirs inherit the obligation — the debt, the fees, the legal liability — automatically. The resort counts on this.

Still in working years (under 55)

Resorts are far more aggressive here. They complete the foreclosure. It stays on your credit for 10 years — worse in practice than most bankruptcies — and comes attached to an active civil judgment. Home loans, car financing, business credit: effectively frozen for a decade.

What comes after foreclosure — the part most owners never anticipate
Post-Foreclosure
Stage 6
Resort files a deficiency judgment in court
They don't stop at foreclosure. They return to court for a deficiency judgment — all future maintenance fees owed for the remaining life of the contract. Typically $10,000–$40,000 or more. Contract terms favor the resort. Judges grant these routinely. It shows on your credit separately as an active civil lawsuit you lost.
Judgment Stage
Stage 7
No exit company will accept your case at this point
Once an active judgment or foreclosure is on record, virtually no timeshare exit firm — including attorneys who specialize in this area — will take your case. The cost to reverse a judgment far exceeds what any firm can profitably charge. The clean exit window closes well before this stage.
Enforcement
Stage 8
Wage garnishment and property liens
With a judgment in hand, resort attorneys move to garnish your wages — your employer withholds a portion of every paycheck and sends it directly to the resort. Simultaneously they file liens against your home and any property you own. You cannot sell, refinance, or leverage anything with an active lien in place.
The Exit
The alternative
A proper exit closes every one of these doors permanently
None of the above can proceed once your name is legally off the deed and off the resort's records. That is the only outcome that ends this completely — not ignoring it, not making partial payments, not waiting. A clean, documented transfer removes all standing the resort has to pursue you. That is exactly what this process is designed to accomplish.
