Resort Relentless Pursuit
From the first missed payment to garnished wages — what this looks like, start to finish

A lot of owners assume that simply stopping payments is an option. It is not. The moment you miss a payment, the resort begins automated calls, certified letters, and past-due notices. Within 30 days all three credit bureaus are notified simultaneously — most owners see a 100–150 point credit drop almost immediately. The account is then sold to collections, which adds a second independent negative mark. Formal foreclosure notices follow. None of this is a bluff.

Where It Splits — Based on Your Age & Profile

Near or at Retirement (55+)

Resorts often skip full foreclosure — not out of mercy, but strategy. They destroy the credit, keep collections active, and wait. A timeshare is real property attached to your estate. When you pass, your heirs inherit the obligation — the debt, the fees, the legal liability — automatically.

Still in Working Years (Under 55)

Resorts are far more aggressive here. They complete the foreclosure. It stays on your credit for 10 years — worse in practice than most bankruptcies — and comes attached to an active civil judgment. Home loans, car financing, business credit: effectively frozen for a decade.

What Comes After — The Part Most Owners Never Anticipate
Stage 6

Deficiency Judgment Filed in Court

They don't stop at foreclosure. They return to court for a deficiency judgment — all future maintenance fees owed for the remaining life of the contract. Typically $10,000–$40,000 or more. Judges grant these routinely. It shows on your credit separately as an active civil lawsuit you lost.

Stage 7

No Exit Company Will Accept Your Case

Once an active judgment or foreclosure is on record, virtually no timeshare exit firm — including attorneys who specialize in this area — will take your case. The cost to reverse a judgment far exceeds what any firm can profitably charge. The clean exit window closes well before this stage.

Stage 8

Wage Garnishment & Property Liens

With a judgment in hand, resort attorneys move to garnish your wages — your employer withholds a portion of every paycheck and sends it directly to the resort. Simultaneously they file liens against your home and any property you own. You cannot sell, refinance, or leverage anything with an active lien in place.

The Window to Exit Cleanly Is Still Open

None of the above can proceed once your name is legally off the deed and off the resort's records. That is the only outcome that ends this completely — not ignoring it, not making partial payments, not waiting. A clean, documented release removes all standing the resort has to pursue you.
