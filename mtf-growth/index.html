Your Maintenance Fee Grows 8% Every Year
Outpacing inflation — and what the resort never mentioned
Consumer Bill of Rights · Right to Be Informed
An 8% annual escalation rate on a perpetual obligation fundamentally changes the financial commitment you are making. This rate must be disclosed. The phrase "maintenance fees may increase" does not satisfy this obligation.
8% Annual Growth — Documented Across the Industry

The average U.S. timeshare maintenance fee increases at approximately 8% per year. This is not a projection — it is a documented historical average confirmed by ARDA's own industry research, with the average fee rising from $731 in 2010 to $1,480 in 2024.

Source — ARDA State of the Vacation Timeshare Industry 2024: Average annual maintenance fee rose from $731 (2010) → $1,120 (2017) → $1,480 (2024). Compound annual growth rate: approximately 5.1% officially — with industry insiders citing 7–9% as the common range owners experience at the resort level.
Source — Vacation Ownership Consultants 2026 Industry Report: "Near 40% increase in four years [2020–2024]. The industry's own explanation is that inflation and rising insurance costs tied to natural disasters drove the sharpest increases in 2022 through 2024."
How This Compares to Inflation

The long-term average U.S. inflation rate is approximately 2.5–3.5%. Maintenance fees growing at 8% annually outpace inflation by 4.5 to 5.5 percentage points every year. That gap compounds over time — meaning your maintenance fee burden grows significantly faster than your other cost-of-living expenses.
