Heirs & Estate Liability

What your family inherits — and why it never ends

This ownership does not end when you do.

A timeshare is deeded real property. When you pass, it transfers automatically to your estate — and your heirs inherit every obligation attached to it.

What Your Heirs Inherit

🏛 Property Taxes

As deeded real estate, property taxes attach to the ownership and continue after death — charged against the estate before distribution.

🏨 HOA / Maintenance Fees — Forever

Maintenance fees are an HOA obligation tied to the deed. Your heirs must either pay them or face the same collections, foreclosure, and credit destruction you would have faced.

♾️ The Perpetuity Clause

The contract has no end date. It was designed to transfer in perpetuity — generation to generation. Your children can pass it to their children. This was almost certainly never explained at the time of purchase.

The Maintenance Fee Trap — 8% Per Year

Maintenance fees increase an average of 8% per year. This is where the real financial damage compounds — and it was almost never disclosed at the sales presentation.

How they sold it: The salesperson focused your attention on the purchase price or the mortgage payment — numbers that felt fixed and manageable.

What they didn't say: The maintenance fee — not the mortgage — is the obligation that never ends, never decreases, and increases every year regardless of whether you use it or not.
