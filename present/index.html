Consumer Rights
You have rights
as a consumer.
In 1962, President Kennedy established
5 Consumer Bill of Rights.
Right 1 of 5
The Right to Safety
You have the right to be protected from products and services that are hazardous to your financial health and wellbeing.
This includes financial safety — not just physical safety.
Right 2 of 5
The Right to Choose
You have the right to access what you own — at fair and competitive terms.
By not disclosing that non-owners can book most dates with 1–7 days notice — often at a lower cost than your annual maintenance fees alone — they limited your ability to make an informed choice about how to use what you own.
Were you ever told that non-owners can book most dates with as little as 1–7 days notice — often at a lower cost than your annual maintenance fees?
Right 3 of 5
The Right to Be Heard
You have the right to formally raise a concern and receive a fair response — without being forced into litigation or arbitration.
Owners deserve a formal process to review their situation and reach a reasonable resolution directly with the resort.
Were you ever given a formal process to review your situation and reach a reasonable resolution directly with the resort?
Right 4 of 5
The Right to Be Informed
You have the right to receive sufficient information before making an informed choice — including full disclosure of all costs, terms, and obligations.
Prior to signing, the resort was required to show you the true long-term cost of ownership — in writing.
Before you signed — did they show you what this timeshare would actually cost over 10, 20, and 30 years?
Right 5 of 5
The Right to Service
You have the right to courtesy, responsiveness, and products or services that perform exactly as claimed.
If the resort failed to deliver on what was promised — in usage, availability, or service — that's a violation of this right.
Did the timeshare perform the way it was presented to you on the day you purchased?
