There Is No Secondary Market
The resale myth — and what your timeshare is actually worth
Consumer Bill of Rights · Right to Choose
If you were told — or even allowed to believe — that you could resell your timeshare, your right to choose was compromised. A free choice requires accurate information. The absence of a resale market is material to any purchase decision.
The Resale Myth

During the sales presentation, you may have been told — directly or by implication — that your timeshare holds value and can be resold. This is false. The timeshare resale market is effectively non-functional. Per industry research, 40% of owners who attempt to sell their timeshare face complete market illiquidity — meaning they cannot find a buyer at any price.

eBay Test: Search for your resort on eBay right now. You will find listings for the same resort, same point values — listed for $1. Some of these listings have been active for years with zero bids. The resort sold you a product that can be purchased by anyone for $1 on the open market. That is the true market value of your timeshare.

Meanwhile, resorts have spent $1.2 billion on marketing in a single year (2022) to replace the owners exiting. They are not concerned about your resale value — only their next sale.

Why Resorts Don't Disclose This

If a salesperson told you "this timeshare has no resale value and cannot be sold," you would walk out of the presentation. So they don't say it. They talk about trading, exchanging, renting — anything that creates the impression of value. None of those options represent resale liquidity. The FTC has cited timeshare resale misrepresentation as one of the top consumer fraud categories in the travel industry.

Misrepresenting or failing to disclose the absence of a secondary resale market for timeshare products is a violation of FTC Act §5 and State Unfair and Deceptive Acts and Practices (UDAP) statutes, carrying a potential fine of $50,000.
The resort takes this financial exposure into consideration when negotiating your release and possible compensation. This violation becomes part of your documented case file.
