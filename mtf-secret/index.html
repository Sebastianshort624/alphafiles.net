Consumer Bill of Rights · Right to Be Informed
You have the right to receive all information required to make an informed financial commitment — including but not limited to total annual expenses. The maintenance fee is your largest ongoing cost. It must be disclosed in full.
The Distraction Strategy

The sales presentation focused heavily on the purchase price — the big number. They negotiated with you, offered discounts, made you feel like you won something. Meanwhile, the real ongoing financial commitment — the maintenance fee — was mentioned briefly if at all.

Here is what that strategy concealed: In the first 10 years of ownership, most timeshare owners pay the same amount in maintenance fees alone as they paid for the timeshare in the first place. The purchase was the one-time cost. The maintenance fee is forever — and it grows every year.
