Most owners do not know there are only four ways out of a timeshare — and three of them either don't work or work against you. Here is the complete picture.
1
The Resort Takes It Back
RARE
Some resorts offer a voluntary surrender or "deed-in-lieu" program. However, this is extremely rare — and resorts will almost never accept it if there is an unpaid mortgage or delinquent maintenance fees. If you qualify, it typically provides no compensation and requires fees of its own.
2
Sell It — The Scam
DOES NOT WORK
A large portion of owners unfortunately pay companies that claim they can sell the timeshare on a secondary market. There is no secondary market. You can find the exact same resort — paid off — listed for $1 on eBay and sites like RedWeek or TUG (Timeshare Users Group) sitting unsold for years.
⚠️ You Cannot Sell Something Nobody Wants to Buy
These "resale" companies collect upfront fees — often $2,000–$5,000 — then do nothing. Many are under investigation by state attorneys general. If anyone tells you they have a buyer waiting: that is a scam.

3B. Timeshare Attorneys
Attorneys send letters threatening lawsuits. Resorts are sometimes responsive — but often not.
✗They rarely call the resort directly — just write letters.
✗Cases can go to court — you pay additional fees regardless of outcome.
✗No law group will petition a single dollar of your refund. Many settlements require you to still pay remaining maintenance fees.
✗Cannot guarantee a refund of their fee — each case is submitted individually.
✗Expensive.
3C. DIY / Teach-Yourself Services
These services teach you to send letters yourself, go through the resort's own customer service process, and hope for the best.
✗Hit or miss — entirely dependent on which resort representative you reach.
✗Takes years. You do all the work as the owner.
✗No money-back guarantee. Fees are non-refundable.
